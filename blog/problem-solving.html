Elegantly solved a problem.

First I’ll just state I don’t think I have elegantly solved a problem, but problems have been solved through curiosity on how things work. I did have a problem though with adding someones scores to their name using dot notation. Firstly I started by guessing, (like I always do) then by trial and error. When testing (using the npm test [insert-test-name] the error that comes up gives you a lot of detail. At first I had no idea what any of this sh!t meant, but then one of the tutors gave a talk at 2pm and no one was asking questions so I just had to make it less awkward by asking, when something comes back as “undefined” what does that mean and how do I know where the error is occurring. Gerard (the host of this “seminar”) went through how to identify what was “expected” and what was “received”. That in itself is a great starting point so you can go back and make certain adjustments to your code. Then trial and error to the end baby! I have learnt that making mistakes is actually a really good stepping stone, if you make a mistake you need to find where your issue has occurred. When you know where the issue is occurring you have a more specific research starting point as opposed to researching a topic as a whole and then trying to learn everything about a topic.
