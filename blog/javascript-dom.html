Explain control flow and loops using an example process

Control flow

Is the flow of control, where the computer uses instructions it has been given. It follows the instructions in order from the first line of instructions to the last line of instructions (note: you can change this if you have an instruction that tells the computer to change its course).
An example of Control Flow:
You want a quick breakfast (toast) before work.
Your instructions would be to:
1. Get your bread
2. Insert your bread into toaster
3. Press start
4. When toast is finished toasting, grab your toast.

By making your breakfast in this order you have followed the flow of control (Control Flow).

Loops

A loop is when you have a set of instruction(s) that you want to repeat itself until you want it to finish. Example: If say in a perfect world you wanted to lose 10kgs in a day and in this world you could lose 1kg doing 1 press-up. Your instructions to lose 10kg in a day would be to: “Do 1 press up” 10 times.
With a loop, this instruction would repeat itself:
Do 1 press up
Do 1 press up
Do 1 press up
Do 1 press up
Do 1 press up
Do 1 press up
Do 1 press up
Do 1 press up
Do 1 press up
Do 1 press up

And the loop would finish once you have done 10 “Do 1 press ups” because you told yourself you only wanted to lose 10kgs that day, therefore doing 10 press ups = losing 10kgs.
